# Healthcare-Revenue-Analysis---2026
Interactive Excel Dashboard analyzing 10k patient records (2019-2024) to identify revenue drivers and operational trends.
Healthcare Revenue & Operations Analysis - 2026
Project Overview:
This project features a comprehensive analysis of a healthcare dataset containing 10,000 patient records (2019-2024). Leveraging my extensive background in healthcare quality and operations, I transformed raw data into an interactive Executive Dashboard. This tool allows stakeholders to visualize revenue trends, patient demographics, and billing consistency across various medical conditions and insurance providers.

Technical Toolkit:
Data Cleaning: Standardized CSV data into a structured Excel Table format for analysis.

Pivot Tables & Modeling: Aggregated complex metrics including Total Revenue, Patient Volume, and Average Billing per condition.

Advanced Visualization: Created a dynamic interface using Slicers (Gender, Insurance Provider) and Combo Charts (Dual-axis Bar and Line charts).

Reporting: Developed a professional layout with a focus on "clean" data presentation (removing gridlines, field buttons, and technical clutter).

Strategic Insights & Observations:
As a Healthcare Quality professional with over two decades of experience, I have identified the following key operational trends:

Revenue Drivers: Chronic conditions such as Obesity and Diabetes represent the highest cumulative billing totals, suggesting a strategic opportunity for targeted wellness and preventative care programs.

Billing Quality: Average billing remains remarkably consistent across all major insurance providers (Medicare, Blue Cross, Aetna, etc.), indicating strong internal adherence to standardized billing protocols and RCM (Revenue Cycle Management) quality.

Predictable Volume: The stable admission rate across the 5-year study period (2019-2024) suggests that the facility can maintain high-quality care through consistent staffing and resource allocation.

Operational Recommendation: Future analysis should investigate "Unknown" insurance categories to further minimize revenue leakage and improve claim submission accuracy.
